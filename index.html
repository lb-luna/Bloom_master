Step-by-step — adding to Shopify

  2. 2
    Once you have specs, go to Shopify Admin → Products → click on a product
  3. 3
    Scroll to "Metafields" at the bottom of the product page (if not visible, go to Settings → Custom data → Products and add metafields for each measurement)
  4. 4
    Alternatively, add measurements directly into the product description using a simple formatted table — faster with no developer needed
  5. 5
    Repeat for every frame in the range

How to display the measurements

Standard industry format: 52 □ 18 — 140 (lens width □ bridge — temple). Add lens height separately as it's particularly important for progressive lens wearers (optical frames).

What each measurement means

  • Lens width: horizontal width of one lens at its widest point (mm). Typical: 48–56mm
  • Bridge width: gap between the two lenses at the nose (mm). Typical: 14–24mm
  • Temple length: hinge to tip of arm (mm). Common: 135, 140, 145mm
  • Total frame width: full width across the front including rims
  • Lens height: vertical height of lens — must be 30mm+ for progressive lenses

Note on existing size guide

The Bloom site already has a size guide — this is a good start. But buyers need per-frame measurements on each product page, not just a general guide. Each frame has different dimensions.

Done when: Every product page shows at minimum: lens width, bridge width, temple length, and total frame width. Lens height on all optical frames. Measurements visible without scrolling or clicking away.

Add UV protection + lens details to all sun frames
UV400, lens category (Cat 1–4), tint colour, polarised yes/no. Critical for Australian buyers.
Critical — sun range ~45 min (once specs received) ▾

What to add to each sun frame page

  1. 1
    UV rating — "UV400 protection" (blocks all UVA + UVB rays up to 400nm wavelength)
  2. 2
    UVA + UVB — "100% UVA and UVB protection" (confirm with supplier)
  3. 3
    Lens category — Cat 1 (very light tint, fashion), Cat 2 (medium, variable light), Cat 3 (dark, general use, driving), Cat 4 (very dark, extreme sun — NOT for driving)
  4. 4
    Polarised — Yes or No. Polarised lenses reduce glare from reflective surfaces (water, roads, glass). A frequently searched filter.
  5. 5
    Lens tint + what it's ideal for — e.g. "Grey tint — true colour perception, ideal for driving and everyday use"
  6. 6
    Lens material — e.g. polycarbonate, CR-39

Lens tint colour guide

  • Grey/Smoke: true colour, minimal distortion, great all-rounder
  • Brown/Amber: enhances contrast, warm tones, great for driving
  • Green: natural colour rendering, reduces eye strain
  • Pink/Rose: fashion tint, indoor/low light, not for intense sun
  • Blue/Mirror: fashion tint, reduces glare in bright conditions

Why this matters in Australia

Australia has some of the world's highest UV levels. Australian buyers are highly aware of UV protection — it's a genuine health concern, not just a feature. Missing UV data signals to buyers that the product may be fashion-only with no real protection. This alone can block a sale.

Done when: Every sun frame page clearly states UV400 protection, lens category, and polarised status. Tint description explains what it's best for.

Note: Only state "UV400" and "100% UVA+UVB" if the supplier has confirmed this. Under Australian Consumer Law, product claims must be accurate. If you don't have this confirmed, write "UV protection — full details on request" temporarily until confirmed.

Add lens suitability info to all optical frames
Lens height (mm), single vision / progressive / bifocal suitability, "frame only" note.
Critical — optical range ~30 min ▾

What to add to each optical frame page

  1. 1
    Lens height in mm — the vertical height of the lens aperture. This determines progressive lens compatibility. Must be 30mm+ for progressives, ideally 35mm+.
  2. 2
    Lens suitability statement — e.g. "Suitable for: Single vision lenses · Progressive lenses · Bifocal lenses" (circle/list whichever applies based on lens height)
  3. 3
    Frame only note — add clearly: "This listing is for the frame only. Bring your frame to any optical store or optometrist for lens fitting."
  4. 4
    Frame size label — Narrow / Medium / Wide. Buyers with prescription glasses know their face width and need this to shortlist frames quickly.

Progressive lens rule of thumb

  • Lens height <28mm: single vision only
  • Lens height 28–32mm: single vision, some progressives (short corridor)
  • Lens height 32–35mm: suitable for most progressives
  • Lens height 35mm+: ideal for all progressives and bifocals

Many of Bloom's target customers (40–60) will be progressive lens wearers — presbyopia (needing reading glasses) typically begins around 40–45. This information is genuinely decision-critical for a significant portion of your buyers.

Done when: All optical frame pages clearly state lens height, which prescription types the frame accommodates, and that it's a frame-only purchase.

How to build a UTM link

  1. 1
    Go to Google Campaign URL Builder (link below)
  2. 2
    Enter your destination URL (e.g. bloomeyewear.com)
  3. 3
    Fill in: Source (where the traffic comes from), Medium (the channel type), Campaign (which campaign/push)
  4. 4
    Copy the generated URL and use it everywhere

UTM examples for Bloom

  • Instagram bio link: source=instagram, medium=social, campaign=bio_link
  • Welcome email link: source=klaviyo, medium=email, campaign=welcome_series
  • Meta ad: source=meta, medium=paid_social, campaign=asc_prospecting
  • Influencer post link: source=instagram, medium=influencer, campaign=sarah_april26

Why UTMs matter

Without UTMs, GA4 reports all these sessions as "direct" traffic — meaning you have no idea if a sale came from Instagram, an email, or an influencer post. You'd be spending on ads and posting content with no way to prove what's working. UTMs take 2 minutes per link and give you complete attribution visibility.

Instagram bio tip

Use a link-in-bio tool like Linktree or Later's Link in Bio so you can have multiple tagged links (to Sun collection, Optical collection, Face Shape Guide etc.) and swap them without changing your bio. Each link gets its own UTM.

The 7-day rule — critical

Every time you edit a campaign (change budget, swap creative, adjust targeting), Meta resets the learning phase. During the learning phase, CPAs are 20–50% higher than post-learning. Discipline = let it run without touching it for 7 days. Only check the numbers — don't change anything.

What to check at day 7

  • Which creative has the best CTR (click-through rate)?
  • Which creative has the lowest cost per link click?
  • Has the campaign exited the learning phase? (Shown in Ads Manager)
  • Only then: pause the 1–2 lowest-performing creatives and add new ones
